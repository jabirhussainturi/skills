# Anti-slop pass

Applied to the canonical draft **every time**, before the voice profile is even
considered. These rules don't make writing sound like *you* — they stop it sounding
like a language model. Voice (`voice.md`) is layered on top.

## Banned openings

No throat-clearing. Cut these on sight:

- "In today's fast-paced world…", "In the ever-evolving landscape of…"
- "Have you ever wondered…", "Picture this:"
- "As a developer, you know that…"
- Any opening that restates the title or defines the obvious before saying anything.

Open on a concrete claim, a specific moment, a real number, or a sharp question that
isn't rhetorical filler.

## Banned vocabulary and tics

- **Words**: delve, leverage (as a verb), seamless, robust, elevate, unlock, harness,
  realm, landscape, tapestry, testament, game-changer, supercharge, effortless,
  cutting-edge, plethora, myriad.
- **Constructions**:
  - "It's not just X, it's Y." (and "not only… but also…")
  - Rule-of-three everywhere ("fast, reliable, and scalable").
  - "Whether you're a beginner or an expert…"
  - "Let's dive in." / "Let's explore." / "Without further ado."
  - Empty hedges: "it's worth noting that", "needless to say", "at the end of the day".
  - Summary sentences that just restate the paragraph above.

## Rhythm

- **Vary sentence length.** LLM prose trends toward uniform medium-length sentences.
  Mix short punches with longer lines. A one-word paragraph is allowed.
- **Vary paragraph length.** No wall of three-sentence paragraphs in a row.
- Don't end every section with a tidy moral. Let some sections just stop.

## Substance over abstraction

- Prefer the concrete: a real example, a real number, a real failure, a code snippet.
- If a sentence would be equally true of any topic, cut it.
- Show the tradeoff, not just the upside. Real writing admits costs.
- Don't explain what the reader already knows to pad length.

## Structure

- Headings are descriptive, not cute ("How the cache invalidates", not "The magic").
- No "Conclusion" heading that summarizes; end where the argument ends.
- Lists only for genuinely parallel items. If items aren't parallel, write prose.

## Final read

Before showing the canonical, re-read it once and ask: *would a knowledgeable human
who knows this topic believe a person wrote this?* If any paragraph reads as generic
filler, cut or sharpen it. The voice profile makes it *theirs*; this pass makes it
*human*.
